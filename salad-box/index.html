Salad Box

Eat more salad

Eating vegetables is healthy. Salad Box makes it easy.

Salad Box is a combination of resilient vegetables, prepped in advance to save you time. And it’s not just for salads anymore!

Why Salad Box?

  • Save time by washing and chopping all at once.
  • Eat more greens with your veggies always prepped and ready.
  • Hearty versatile vegetables provide a base for scrambles, stir frys, sandwiches, soups, and more!
  • Green on the go! Road trips, weekend getaways, picnics— have Salad Box, will travel.
  • Waste less food because veggies stay fresh in the box instead of wilting in your fridge.

Instructions

Do all your prep once and in about an hour you’ll have everything you need for the whole week.

  1. Wash ingredients with fruit & veggie wash
  2. Chop, chop, chop and massage the kale with olive oil (since kale can be a bit tough, massaging it makes it much more edible)
  3. Layer the veggies in the box

Optional additions include: radish, carrots, radicchio, shaved brussle sprouts

Important: do not add dressing to the box!

(Furry arms optional)

Enjoy!

Salad Box keeps your veggies fresh all week. This unique combination of ingredients are a hearty base salads, omlettes, soups, sandwiches, stir fries—whatever your creativity can cook up!

For a quick salad, serve a few scoops and top your favorite salad toppers. Here are some ideas: cucumbers, avocado, olives, pine nuts, pumpkin seeds, cashews, shaved almonds, wasabi peas, tortilla strips, shredded cheese, or sprouts. Make it heartier with kidney, cannellini, or garbanzo beans.

Dress to impress. Try mixing a heaping scoop of hummus, olive oil, and a vinegar in a mason jar for a quick and hearty dressing that will also keep all week.

Do’s

  • Substitue or add your own ingredients: carrots, radishes, radicchio, shaved brussle sprouts. Remember to use vegetables that are hearty and don’t brown easily.
  • Spice things up by using different oils in your dressing. For example, sesame and peanut oil can take your salad in a whole new direction. Arigato!
  • Throw nothing away! Toss soggy leftovers into the blender for a quick and distinctive dressing.

Dont’s

  • Keep your box undressed! Dress your salad when you serve it. If there are leftovers, these veggies will make it through the night (can’t vouch for your toppings). Beyond that, you’ll end up with soggy salad that you’ll have to toss. No one wants to toss a soggy salad.
  • All the veggies in the box should be able to be sauteéd. Keep other ingredients that don’t cook well (like sprouts) separate. This makes Salad Box more versatile.
